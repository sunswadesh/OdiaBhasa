Odia Grammar (ଓଡ଼ିଆ ବ୍ୟାକରଣ)

Basic Sentence Structure

Odia, like many other Indo-Aryan languages, typically follows a Subject-Object-Verb (SOV) word order in its sentences. This is different from English, which usually follows a Subject-Verb-Object (SVO) order.

Let's look at an example:

English (SVO): Sita reads a book.

Odia (SOV): ସୀତା ବହି ପଢ଼େ ।

(Roman transliteration: Sītā bahi paḍhe.)

Breakdown:

  • ସୀତା (Sītā) - Subject (who is performing the action)
  • ବହି (bahi) - Object (what the action is performed on)
  • ପଢ଼େ (paḍhe) - Verb (the action)

Understanding this basic word order is a key first step to forming your own sentences in Odia.
