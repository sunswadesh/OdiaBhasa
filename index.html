Chapter: Basic Introductions

Vocabulary / Key Phrases:

English Odia Script Roman Transliteration Image
What's your name? (formal) ଆପଣଙ୍କର ନାମ କ’ଣ? Āpaṇaṅkara nāma kaṇa?
My name is ... ମୋର ନାମ ... Mora nāma ...
Where are you from? ଆପଣ କେଉଁଠାରୁ ଆସିଛନ୍ତି? Āpaṇa keũṭhāru āsichanti?
I'm from ... ମୁଁ ... ରୁ ଆସିଛି । Mũ ... ru āsichi.

Sample Conversations:

Person A (En): Hello! What's your name?

Person A (Odia): ନମସ୍କାର! ଆପଣଙ୍କର ନାମ କ’ଣ? (Namascāra! Āpaṇaṅkara nāma kaṇa?)

Person B (En): My name is Gita.

Person B (Odia): ମୋର ନାମ ଗୀତା । (Mora nāma Gītā.)


Person A (En): Where are you from?

Person A (Odia): ଆପଣ କେଉଁଠାରୁ ଆସିଛନ୍ତି? (Āpaṇa keũṭhāru āsichanti?)

Person B (En): I'm from Cuttack.

Person B (Odia): ମୁଁ କଟକରୁ ଆସିଛି । (Mũ Kaṭakaru āsichi.)

Odia Grammar (ଓଡ଼ିଆ ବ୍ୟାକରଣ)

Basic Sentence Structure

Odia, like many other Indo-Aryan languages, typically follows a Subject-Object-Verb (SOV) word order in its sentences. This is different from English, which usually follows a Subject-Verb-Object (SVO) order.

Let's look at an example:

English (SVO): Sita reads a book.

Odia (SOV): ସୀତା ବହି ପଢ଼େ ।

(Roman transliteration: Sītā bahi paḍhe.)

Breakdown:

  • ସୀତା (Sītā) - Subject (who is performing the action)
  • ବହି (bahi) - Object (what the action is performed on)
  • ପଢ଼େ (paḍhe) - Verb (the action)

Understanding this basic word order is a key first step to forming your own sentences in Odia.
